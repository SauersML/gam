TO: A mathematician with no limits on depth, patience, or invention.
RE: Find the optimal spline. Or whatever should replace the spline.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
THE PROBLEM
----------------------------------------------------------------------

We observe n points (x_i, y_i), x_i in R^d (d from 1 to a few dozen,
n from thousands to hundreds of millions), y_i noisy. We want to
estimate a smooth function f, with honest uncertainty, inside a larger
model (other terms, link functions, non-Gaussian likelihoods,
hyperparameters chosen by marginal likelihood). The estimator must be
computed on physical machines: finite memory, deep memory hierarchies,
wide parallelism, arithmetic that is fast for some operations and slow
for others, and floating point that rounds.

Today this is done with splines and kernel methods: choose a smoothness
functional, derive its minimizer, evaluate the resulting basis
pointwise, and pay whatever linear algebra falls out. Every named
construction in the literature fixed the mathematics first and accepted
the computation that resulted. The reverse direction — and every
direction orthogonal to both — is essentially unexplored. We do not
believe the optimum has been found. We are not convinced the right
*question* has been posed. That is your opening.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
WHAT "OPTIMAL" MUST ANSWER TO
----------------------------------------------------------------------

Judge any candidate against all of these at once, as one object:

  1. STATISTICAL: risk under honest assumption classes; correct
     uncertainty; graceful behavior where data is sparse and beyond
     its range; invariances that match the problem, not the basis;
     no hidden bias against structure the user cares about (e.g.
     large-scale trends).

  2. COMPUTATIONAL: total cost to fit, to evaluate, to differentiate
     with respect to everything (inputs, coefficients,
     hyperparameters), and to certify — as functions of n, d, and
     accuracy. Constants matter. Memory traffic matters. Parallel
     depth matters. Worst case and typical case both matter.

  3. TRUSTWORTHY: every approximation must be certifiable a
     posteriori — the algorithm must know when it is wrong and have
     a correct fallback. Accuracy by fiat is disqualifying.

  4. COMPOSABLE: it must live inside larger models — penalized
     likelihoods, multiple terms, hyperparameter optimization that
     needs derivatives of the whole fit. An estimator that is fast
     alone but opaque to differentiation or marginal likelihood is
     not a solution.

If these pull against each other, prove it: exhibit the frontier and
the trade-off theorem. If they do not, exhibit the object that
achieves them simultaneously. Either result is an answer. Asserted
tension without proof is not.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
FREEDOMS
----------------------------------------------------------------------

You are not required to produce a kernel, a basis expansion, an RKHS,
a variational problem, or anything called a spline. Those are
artifacts of how humans found the known solutions. Discard any of
them. Discard all of them. You may redefine the estimand, the noise
model, the notion of smoothness, the notion of evaluation, or the
boundary between "model" and "algorithm" — provided you state what
you changed and prove the replacement answers the same practical
need. If the best object is adaptive, randomized, implicit, defined
only through an algorithm, or stranger than that: fine. The
requirements above are the only contract.

Do not assume current hardware is permanent; do assume physics —
locality, parallelism, finite precision — is.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
WHAT AN ANSWER LOOKS LIKE
----------------------------------------------------------------------

  - The construction, stated precisely.
  - Proofs of its statistical properties, with assumption classes
    made explicit.
  - Matching lower bounds, or your best obstruction results: in what
    sense is this optimal, and against what class of alternatives?
    "Best I found" is a progress report, not an answer.
  - The algorithm, with cost and certification analysis, to the
    level a competent engineer could implement without further
    invention.
  - The failure modes, stated plainly: where it loses to existing
    methods, and by how much.

If you discover instead that the question is malposed — that
"optimal spline" dissolves under the right formalization — then say
exactly what dissolves it and what the well-posed successor question
is. That, too, is an answer worth having.

Take whatever path through mathematics you need. We have given you
the constraints we actually have, and nothing else, on purpose.
